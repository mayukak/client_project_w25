Maize and Blue Cupboard

Maize & Blue Cupboard ensures that no student goes hungry at Michigan.

The Cupboard offers free groceries, personal hygiene items, and fresh produce to all students. You can visit multiple times per week — no proof of income required. You can also volunteer or donate to help support your peers in need.

Welcome to Maize & Blue Cupboard

The Maize & Blue Cupboard (MBC) at the University of Michigan is committed to ensuring that all students have access to healthy, nutritious food and essential personal items. MBC provides free groceries, kitchen supplies, and personal care products to students in need, fostering a supportive and inclusive environment.

Grocery Access:

Students can shop for a variety of fresh produce, pantry staples, and frozen items.

Personal Care Items:

Access to hygiene products and household essentials to support overall well-being.

Nutritional Education:

Workshops and resources to promote healthy eating and cooking habits.

Food Security Resources:

Connections to campus and community support services.

How to Access MBC:

Students can visit the cupboard by scheduling an appointment or utilizing walk-in hours. MBC aims to provide a welcoming and judgment-free environment for all.

Location: Betsy Barbour Residence Hall, Ground Floor
